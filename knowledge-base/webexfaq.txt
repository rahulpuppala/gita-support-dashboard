LLM-ready FAQ knowledge base
1) What is this host group for?
This WhatsApp group supports Webex hosts for the Chinmaya Gita Samarpanam event. Hosts coordinate Webex meetings, practice sessions, pacer video usage, OBS recording, and participant setup for the large synchronized Chapter 15 chanting event.
2) Is OBS for recording or streaming?
OBS is for recording, not streaming. Dr Aparna directly confirmed this.
3) Why not just use Webex recording?
Webex has built-in recording, but Ram explained that cloud recording only captures a 5x5 grid, which is too limited for the event scale. That is why OBS is used as the main recording method.
4) Should cloud recording still be enabled in Webex?
Yes. Ram said OBS is Plan A and Webex cloud recording is Plan B / fallback in case OBS fails.
5) How should host and participant audio be handled?
Ram said hosts keep their own volume at max, while participants reduce volume to about 5% to avoid hearing too many voices at once.
6) How will hosts synchronize the pacer video start time?
Ram said the hosts would receive the exact start time through the host WhatsApp coordination group, and hosts would need to stop and restart their video to begin the pacer video at the correct time.
7) Can the pacer video be smaller so the host stays centered?
Ameetha said at least half the screen should show the pacer video so it stays clear for participants. Hosts may need to make a small camera or laptop adjustment instead.
8) Do hosts have to install the Webex app?
Yes. Ameetha said all hosts are required to download the app because many Webex features work only in the app. A host also reported that adding the pacer video worked after installing the desktop app, not via the browser.
9) How do hosts add the pacer video?
Download the pacer video locally and add it as a virtual background in the Webex app. Dr Aparna also gave the sequence: click your small window, open the menu, change virtual background, and add the pacer video without audio.
10) What if I do not see the option to add a virtual background?
The most reliable fix from the group is to install and use the Webex desktop app instead of the browser version.
11) Are hosts also participants?
Yes. Ameetha explicitly said every host is also a participant and must register as a participant too.
12) Do hosts also chant?
The group strongly suggests that hosts are expected to chant too, but the clearest wording in the excerpt came from a participant rather than an admin. For LLM use, phrase it as: hosts are generally expected to chant as participants, since every host is also a participant.
13) How do people sign up to become a host?
Ameetha shared official host registration forms for India and non-India hosts. Registered hosts receive a Webex license and access to training.
14) How do people register as participants?
Ameetha shared official participant registration links and stated that hosts must also register as participants.
15) What training is available for hosts?
There are weekly Saturday host training/practice sessions, generally around 10:00 a.m. ET, plus tech-support sessions and one-on-one setup help.
16) If I already know Webex and OBS, do I still need to attend Saturday training?
The group guidance suggests Saturday sessions are for setup/testing, especially for new hosts. If already comfortable, attendance may be less necessary, but the strongest supported statement is that hosts are encouraged to attend at least one training session.
17) Where can I get one-on-one technical help?
VNR posted dedicated tech-support Webex sessions for hosts who need one-on-one help configuring Webex and OBS.
18) Where are the setup instructions and links?
Ameetha and Pramod repeatedly pointed hosts to the group description, group info, pinned document, cheatsheet, and tutorial links.
19) Where can hosts find participant information?
Goutham said hosts should log in to the portal, click Meetings at the top, then open the meeting details to access participant information and related links. He explicitly clarified that this is not in the dashboard.
20) Is participant info in the dashboard?
No. Goutham explicitly said it is not in the dashboard; it is under the Meetings menu at the top.
21) Can hosts automatically create WhatsApp groups through the portal?
No. Goutham said WhatsApp does not allow group creation via programming in this workflow. It can only connect to existing groups, so hosts are expected to create their own groups.
22) Can hosts schedule meetings themselves?
Yes. Goutham said meetings can be created and scheduled anytime by the host via the Webex portal, and hosts can invite participants themselves.
23) Can hosts choose mutually convenient practice times with participants?
Yes. Goutham’s response implies hosts have flexibility because they can create/schedule meetings themselves and invite participants directly.
24) Are all hosts assigned participants immediately?
No. Ameetha said assignments are gradual. Some hosts may initially see only a few participants, and more are assigned as registrations happen.
25) Will hosts get more participants later?
Yes. Ameetha said more participants would be assigned over time as registrations come in. Goutham also later said hosts may get a new meeting and new participants that will stay with them until the event.
26) Can hosts reuse the same WhatsApp group if they get new participants?
Yes. Goutham said hosts can reuse the same WhatsApp group, remove old members, and add the new assigned participants.
27) Can hosts get participant phone numbers and emails?
Yes, participant information is available through the portal’s Meetings area according to Goutham.
28) What name should participants display on screen?
Goutham said the name on the screen should be the one they registered with.
29) Can hosts request specific participants they already know?
Yes. The chat indicates there is a request form for this workflow. Goutham said just filling the request form is sufficient.
30) Do hosts need to post requested participant emails again in the group?
No. Goutham said filling the request form is sufficient.
31) How many participants can a host accommodate?
This depends on the host’s system and grid capacity. The group discussed hosts being able to show 49 or 64 participants depending on machine limitations, and that this is tracked for assignment purposes.
32) What is the default host capacity if not updated?
Dr Aparna said that if a host does not update their Webex portal settings, the default is 25.
33) How do hosts increase the number of participants they can be assigned?
Dr Aparna said hosts should sign in to the Webex portal, go to the dashboard, and update the max grid size so they can be assigned more than the default 25.
34) What if a participant says they registered but did not get a confirmation email?
Pramod’s guidance was to provide the name and email address so the team can check.
35) Where should support questions be sent by email?
The clearest email support path mentioned in the group is CGS@chinmayavrindavan.org.
36) How large are OBS recordings likely to be?
Pramod said a 40-minute recording on Mac was about 300–350 MB.
37) Does storage type affect recording performance?
Yes. Pramod said SSD write performance is faster, so storage type can affect recording performance.
38) What should hosts do if OBS does not capture correctly?
The chat suggests hosts should test in advance, compare settings, attend training or tech-support sessions, and escalate for help. One useful practical tip from a host was to set the OBS canvas resolution equal to the display resolution to capture the entire screen correctly.
39) What if OBS on Mac does not capture audio?
This issue was raised, but the excerpt does not show a final canonical fix. Treat this as a support escalation issue rather than a settled FAQ answer.
40) What recording feedback did Goutham give on sample uploads?
Goutham gave concrete review guidance: make the video screen full screen with no side/bottom gaps, move the title to the bottom, and make sure participant audio is audible in the recording. He said hosts can reduce their own volume in OBS audio sources/mixer if needed.
